Tattoo Styles

My Specializations

From sacred geometry to precise linework and powerful blackwork compositions — here you will find the five tattoo styles I specialize in. Each style has its own aesthetics, technique, and impact.

Mandala

Sacred Geometry

Mandalas are symmetrical, circular patterns with deep spiritual meaning. Originally from Hindu and Buddhist traditions, they symbolize the universe, balance, and inner harmony. Each mandala is individually designed — from delicate ornamental details to large-scale compositions.

Symmetrical Spiritual Ornamental Detailed

Geometry

Mathematical Precision

Geometric tattoos use clean lines, exact angles, and mathematical patterns. From simple minimalist shapes to complex sacred geometry compositions — every line must sit perfectly. These tattoos work through their clarity and the tension between simplicity and complexity.

Precise Minimalist Sacred Geometry Clean
Request Geometry →

Dotwork

Dot by Dot

Dotwork tattoos consist of thousands of individual dots that together create shading, textures, and patterns. This technique creates soft transitions and an almost meditative aesthetic. Especially with mandalas and geometric designs, dotwork unfolds its full effect — gentle yet expressive.

Meditative Texture Shading Handwork
Request Dotwork →

Fineline

The Art of the Fine Line

Fineline tattoos are characterized by extremely thin, precise lines. Delicate motifs, graceful ornaments, and minimalist designs — every millimeter counts. Fineline requires the highest concentration and a steady hand. The result: tattoos that are elegant, timeless, and subtle.

Delicate Elegant Timeless Minimal
Request Fineline →

Blackwork

Contrast & Statement

Blackwork uses large-scale black areas and strong contrasts. From tribal-inspired patterns to abstract compositions and ornamental designs — blackwork is bold, striking, and timeless. In combination with dotwork or geometry, unique hybrid styles emerge.

Bold Contrast Statement Large-scale
