3. Data Collection on This Website

Who is responsible for data collection?

Data processing on this website is carried out by the website operator. The contact details can be found in the legal notice.

How do we collect your data?

Your data is collected on the one hand by you providing it to us, e.g. via the contact form. Other data is automatically collected by our IT systems when you visit the website (e.g. browser, operating system, time of page access).

4. Contact Form

If you send us inquiries via the contact form, your information from the inquiry form, including the contact data you provided there, will be stored by us for the purpose of processing the inquiry and in case of follow-up questions. We do not pass on this data without your consent.

The processing of this data is based on Art. 6 Abs. 1 lit. b GDPR if your inquiry is related to the fulfillment of a contract, or on Art. 6 Abs. 1 lit. f GDPR (legitimate interest in processing your inquiry).

5. Hosting

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. Provider is GitHub Inc., 88 Colin P Kelly Jr St, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA. When you visit the website, GitHub automatically collects various log data. Details can be found in the GitHub Privacy Policy.

6. Form Processing

For processing the booking form, we use the Forminit service. When you submit the form, the entered data is transmitted to Forminit for processing. This is based on Art. 6 Abs. 1 lit. b GDPR (contract initiation).

7. Fonts

This site uses the font "Inter Tight" for uniform display. The fonts are stored locally on our server – no connection to Google servers is made. Your IP address is not transmitted to third parties.

8. Your Rights

You have the right at any time to:

– Access your stored data (Art. 15 GDPR)
– Rectification of incorrect data (Art. 16 GDPR)
– Erasure of your data (Art. 17 GDPR)
– Restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
– Data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
– Object to processing (Art. 21 GDPR)

Simply contact us by email.

9. Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority. The competent supervisory authority is the State Commissioner for Data Protection of the federal state in which our company is based.
